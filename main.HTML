Our Research Details

Our group is researching how online learning platforms compare to traditional classroom methods when teaching digital literacy skills to new students. My study is inspired by existing research, like the one about APEC Schools in Marikina Heights, which looked at how adaptive learning systems (a type of online learning) affect junior high school students compared to traditional teaching.

What Our Group is Focusing On:

Effectiveness: I want to find out if online platforms (like interactive websites, apps, or video lessons) are just as good as face-to-face teaching in helping students learn digital skills—things like using computers, navigating the internet, or creating digital content.

Engagement: Some studies suggest that online learning can be more fun and interactive, which might make students more interested and motivated to learn. I want to see if this is true and how it impacts their performance.

Student Outcomes: Are students who learn digitally doing better, worse, or the same as those in traditional classrooms? I’ll look at grades, skill tests, or even surveys to compare results.

Why It Matters:
With more schools using technology, especially after the pandemic, it’s important to know if online learning is really helping students or if sticking to traditional methods is better. This could help teachers and schools decide how to teach digital literacy in the future.
